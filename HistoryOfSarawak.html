About Sarawak

Sarawak is the largest state of Malaysia, located on the island of Borneo. The capital city is Kuching, the economic epicenter of the state. Other major cities include Sibu, Miri, and Bintulu. Sarawak is famous for its many ethnicities and cultures as well as its wild and beautiful rainforests. Its abundant natural resources, strategic location, rapidly improving infrastructure, and forward-thinking state policies make it a magnet for business investors.

History Of Sarawak

Before 1841, Sarawak was loosely governed by Bruneiâ€™s Sultanate, with ties to Portuguese explorers. In 1598, Sultan Tengah was appointed Sultan of Sarawak but was later assassinated. In the 19th century, unrest led local leaders to revolt against Brunei, and British adventurer James Brooke helped suppress the rebellion in 1841, becoming the first Rajah of Sarawak. Brooke expanded Sarawak's territory and developed its infrastructure, which continued under his successors until Japanese occupation in 1941. After World War II, Sarawak became a British Crown Colony. In 1963, the Malaysia Agreement granted Sarawak self-governance and it joined the Federation of Malaysia on 16 September 1963.
