Mulu National Park - UNESCO World Heritage Site

This UNESCO-listed national park is unlike any other place on earth.

A vast network of caves, rivers, waterfalls, and limestone pinnacles may be found in Gunung Mulu National Park, which is tucked away in a lush rainforest and is teeming with life. Gunung Mulu National Park is a must-see whether you're exploring the largest cave room in the world, swimming in the Paku waterfall, trying your nerves on canopy walks, or just admiring the breathtaking natural beauty all around you.

Niah National Park

Step into a land before time at the Niah National Park.

Being a valuable archaeological site, the Great Cave is one of Sarawak's top attractions for good cause. Take a guided tour through the vast system of pathways and caves to gain insight into the life of the area's past occupants and the tales they conveyed through cave paintings some 40,000 years ago.

Miri-Sibuti Coral Reefs National Park

Miri, a city in the north of the island of Borneo, is well-known for its vibrant coral reefs and varied marine life. There are shipwrecks to explore and dive in what is regarded as one of the world's most beautiful and healthiest locations. One of the most popular wrecks to explore is the 100-meter-long Japanese WWII ship known as the Atago Maru, where big barracudas, jacks, and moray eels are regularly spotted. Hard and soft corals flourish in magnificent diversity in terms of the composition of the flora and fauna. During the day, turtles, schooling mackerels, marble rays, napoleon wrasses, groupers, sharks, and leopard sharks congregate. You might even be able to see the huge whale sharks that pass through these seas at specific periods of the year.

Borneo Cultures Museum

With more than 1,000 artefacts showcasing the rich culture and tradition of the indigenous peoples of Borneo and Sarawak, the Borneo Cultures Museum is the second largest museum in Southeast Asia, having only opened to the public in March 2022. No cultural lover should miss this location, which has five levels with various exhibition and function themes.

Padawan Hiking Trails

One of Kuching's best-kept hiking secrets is the Padawan region, which is an hour's drive from the city. Hikers will be in for a treat as they take in the stunning natural surroundings. The majority of Padawan's hiking attractions are waterfall treks. You may interact with the Bidayuh people, learn about their customs, and get to know them better by coming here.
